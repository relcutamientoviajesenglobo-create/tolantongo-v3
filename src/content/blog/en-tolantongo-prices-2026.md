Admission to Grutas de Tolantongo costs $220 MXN per adult in 2026. Parking is $60 MXN per vehicle. Cabins range from $1,200 to $4,500 MXN per night and camping costs $150 MXN per person. A full weekend for a couple requires between $5,000 and $8,500 MXN in cash, since credit or debit cards are not accepted for any service at the park.

## How Much Does Admission to Tolantongo Cost in 2026?

General admission to Grutas de Tolantongo in 2026 costs $220 MXN per adult, $110 MXN per child ages 4 to 11, and is free for children under 4. This ticket includes access to all swimming areas: grottoes, terraced pools, Tunnel of Love, and the river.

### Admission Price Breakdown

| Item | 2026 Price |
|---|---|
| Adult admission (12 years and older) | $220 MXN |
| Child admission (4-11 years) | $110 MXN |
| Children under 4 | Free |
| Parking (car) | $60 MXN |
| Parking (motorcycle) | $30 MXN |
| Parking (large truck/van) | $80 MXN |

Admission is paid once upon arrival and is valid for your entire stay, whether it is a day visit or multiple days if you are staying at the park. There are no additional charges for accessing any of the swimming areas: the grottoes, the 40+ terraced pools, the Tunnel of Love, and the river are all included in the admission price.

Park access hours are 7:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. for day visitors. If you arrive after 5:00 p.m. without a lodging reservation, entry is not allowed. Guests with reservations can arrive until 8:00 p.m.

### Payment Methods

**Cash only.** This is the single most important thing to remember. There are no card terminals for credit or debit cards at any establishment inside Tolantongo: not at the entrance, not at restaurants, not at lodging, not at shops.

The nearest ATM is in Ixmiquilpan, 32 km and 40-50 minutes away. Before leaving Ixmiquilpan, withdraw all the cash you plan to spend. Check our budget table below to calculate how much you need.

## How Much Do Cabins at Tolantongo Cost?

Cabins at Tolantongo range from $1,200 MXN per night for a basic 2-person cabin to $4,500 MXN per night for a premium cabin with a canyon view and capacity for 6 people. Availability varies by season, and booking 2 to 4 weeks in advance is recommended.

### Cabin Types and 2026 Prices

| Cabin Type | Capacity | Price per Night | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | 2 people | $1,200-1,500 MXN | Queen bed, bathroom, blankets |
| Standard | 2-3 people | $1,500-2,000 MXN | Queen + single bed, bathroom, blankets |
| Family | 4-6 people | $2,000-3,500 MXN | 2-3 beds, bathroom, blankets, spacious |
| Premium (canyon view) | 2-4 people | $3,500-4,500 MXN | Terrace with view, king bed, full bathroom |
| Bunk room | 4-8 people | $1,800-2,800 MXN | Bunk beds, shared bathroom, blankets |

### What Cabins Include and Do Not Include

**Included:**
- Bed(s) with blankets and pillows
- Bathroom with shower (thermal hot water)
- Electricity with outlets
- Access to all swimming areas
- Parking

**Not included:**
- Towels (bring your own)
- Toiletries (soap, shampoo)
- Food (there are restaurants inside the park)
- WiFi (no internet in most areas)
- Television (not available in basic or standard cabins)

### Reservations

Reservations are made by phone directly with the Grutas de Tolantongo administration. There is no official online booking system. During peak season (Easter Week, long weekends, Christmas-New Year), it is essential to book at least 3 to 4 weeks in advance. During low season (Monday through Thursday outside of holidays), availability is generally open without a reservation.

Important reservation details:

- A 50% deposit is required to confirm (paid by bank deposit or transfer).
- The remaining 50% is paid upon arrival in cash.
- Cancellations less than 48 hours in advance may forfeit the deposit.
- Check-in: from 2:00 p.m.
- Check-out: before 12:00 p.m.

## How Much Does Camping at Tolantongo Cost?

Camping at Tolantongo costs $150 MXN per person per night, in addition to the $220 MXN general admission. You must bring your own tent and gear. The camping areas have restrooms, showers with thermal water, and access to all swimming areas in the park.

### Camping Price Breakdown

| Item | 2026 Price |
|---|---|
| Camping zone (per person/night) | $150 MXN |
| General admission (paid separately) | $220 MXN adult |
| Tent rental (if you do not bring one) | $300-500 MXN/night |
| Sleeping bag rental | $100-150 MXN/night |

### What the Camping Zone Includes

- Flat space for your tent
- Access to restrooms and showers (thermal hot water)
- Access to all swimming areas
- Nearby parking

### What You Need to Bring for Camping

- Tent (wind-resistant for the canyon)
- Sleeping bag (winter nights drop to 5°C / 41°F)
- Sleeping pad or insulating mat
- Flashlight and spare batteries
- Food and drinks (or money for restaurants)
- Warm clothing for nighttime

Camping is the most affordable overnight option at Tolantongo. A person camping one night spends $370 MXN on admission and camping, while one night in a basic cabin for one person totals $220 MXN admission + $1,200 MXN cabin = $1,420 MXN. The difference is nearly $1,050 MXN per night.

### Save on Food: Bring Your Own

You can bring food and drinks from outside. There is no restriction on bringing food into the park (except glass and alcohol in swimming areas). Many visitors bring:

- Sandwiches prepared at home
- Fresh fruit
- Energy bars and trail mix
- Water in plastic bottles (not glass)

If you bring your own food, you can reduce food expenses to practically zero and use the restaurants only for one hot meal.

## Are There Discounts or Promotions at Tolantongo?

There are no regular discounts or loyalty programs at Grutas de Tolantongo. Prices are fixed and apply equally to all visitors. There are no discounts for students, teachers, seniors, or Hidalgo residents. The only exception is children under 4, who enter free.

Some pricing considerations:

- **Prices do not vary by season**: unlike many tourist destinations, Tolantongo charges the same admission and lodging prices in peak and low season. There is no surcharge during Easter Week or December.
- **There are no all-inclusive packages**: each service is paid separately (admission, parking, lodging, food).
- **No online sales**: there is no official website for purchasing advance tickets or making online reservations.
- **Large groups**: for groups of more than 20 people (schools, companies), it may be possible to negotiate special rates by contacting the park administration directly by phone.

### Tips for Spending Less

1. **Bring everything from home**: biodegradable sunscreen, water shoes, towel, and snacks bought in the city cost 30-50% less than inside the park.
2. **Visit on weekdays**: although prices do not drop, the experience is better with fewer people and you spend the same on gas.
3. **Camp instead of cabins**: savings of $1,050+ MXN per night compared to the cheapest cabin.
4. **Bring prepared food**: 3 homemade meals vs. purchasing them there saves $250-450 MXN per person per day.
5. **Share a car**: with 4 people, transportation from Mexico City drops to $195-355 MXN per person round trip.
6. **Take the bus if traveling alone**: $460-660 MXN per person round trip from Mexico City vs. $780-1,420 MXN driving alone.
